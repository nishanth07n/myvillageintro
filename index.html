Our Village

Built from
earth itself

Ichangoor sits quietly in Tamil Nadu, a village where two ancient crafts define every sunrise — the crackling warmth of brick kilns firing through the night, and the gentle green of paddy fields stretching to the horizon. Here, clay is not just soil; it is livelihood, identity, and artistry.

Every brick that leaves Ichangoor carries the memory of the hands that shaped it — and every harvest season writes a new chapter in a story centuries old.

The Living Craft

Fired by
tradition

The brick industry of Ichangoor is its beating heart. Hundreds of kilns dot the landscape, their chimneys exhaling the slow breath of fire and clay. From raw earth to finished brick, every step is an art passed down through generations.

1

Clay Harvesting

Rich alluvial clay is carefully selected and harvested from the earth — the quality of the soil determines the strength of every brick.

2

Moulding & Drying

Clay is mixed with water, pressed into wooden moulds, and sun-dried for days under the Tamil Nadu sky until firm and pale.

3

Kiln Firing

Stacked in towering kilns, the dried bricks are fired for 48–72 hours at intense heat, transforming raw clay into enduring terracotta.

4

Sorting & Supply

Cooled bricks are graded by quality and dispatched across Tamil Nadu — building homes, temples, and futures.

Our Heritage

Layers of
history

Ichangoor's story is written in brick and grain — a village whose identity has been forged over generations.

Ancient Roots
The First Kilns
The tradition of brick-making in this region stretches back centuries, with local families mastering the art of reading clay and fire — knowledge passed from parent to child without a single written word.
Agricultural Heritage
Fields Fed a Kingdom
Ichangoor's fertile land has nourished generations. The same fields that grow paddy today once supplied grain to regional markets and local temples, weaving the village into the wider fabric of Tamil Nadu's history.
Modern Industry
Brick Capital of the Region
Today, Ichangoor stands as one of the most concentrated brick manufacturing hubs in Tamil Nadu, with hundreds of kilns supplying construction projects across districts — combining ancient craft with modern demand.
Living Today
Tradition Meets Tomorrow
The young generation of Ichangoor grows up between the kiln and the paddy field, carrying forward both identities — proud of where they come from, and building the path forward with the very bricks they make.
